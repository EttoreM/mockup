What are the effect of air pollution on health?

Exposure to air pollution causes both short term and long term effects on health. Air pollution can affect everyone, but those with pre-existing heart or lung conditions, as well as older people and young children, are expetially vulnerable (Find out more here)

It is estimated that 28,000 to 36,000 people die every year in the UK due to long term exposure to man-made air pollution.

Exposure estimates are calculated based on annual average ambient concentration of PM2.5 and combined with a model of how much time people spend at home, indoors (not at home), outdoors and on trasnport.

Particulate matter below 2.5 µm in diameter (PM2.5)

Small pollution particles that can penetrate deep into the lungs. The WHO recomments a target annual average exposure to PM2.5 of 5µg /m3
